<Section icon={BookOpen} title="Understanding Your Progress">

In *Panic Delivery*, your "save file" functions differently than in traditional single-player RPGs. Because the game is a 4-player co-op horror comedy, your progress is split into two distinct categories: session-based mission data and persistent account-wide unlocks.

Understanding how these elements interact is crucial for maintaining your status as a top-tier courier. The system is designed to reward consistent play, even if your team repeatedly fails in the monster-infested zones.

| Data Type | Persistence Level | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Cosmetics** | Permanent | Skins, dances, and emojis unlocked via Challenges. |
| **Shop Items** | Permanent | Weapons and tools bought with Speetchy. |
| **Mission Progress** | Session-based | Current dungeon floor and route completion. |
| **Shared Lives** | Run-based | The 99-life pool resets once the team is wiped out. |

<Callout type="tip">
Focus on completing Challenges early. These unlock the cosmetic items that remain on your account regardless of how many times your team burns through the 99 lives pool.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Managing Your Courier Data">

To ensure your progress is saved, you must rely on the game’s integration with Steam. Since *Panic Delivery* uses procedurally generated dungeons, the game state is constantly updating during your run.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="End Missions Properly">
    Always return to the hub or complete the mission objective before exiting to the main menu to ensure the game registers your rewards.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Steam Cloud">
    Ensure Steam Cloud synchronization is enabled in your Steam library settings for *Panic Delivery* to avoid local save file corruption.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Verify File Integrity">
    If you encounter crashes or missing cosmetic data, use the Steam "Verify integrity of game files" option to repair any corrupted local data.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Forced Closures">
Force-closing the game via Task Manager during a mission can lead to inconsistent synchronization. While your cosmetic unlocks are generally safe, you may lose the Speetchy earned during that specific, incomplete run.
</Callout>

</Section>
